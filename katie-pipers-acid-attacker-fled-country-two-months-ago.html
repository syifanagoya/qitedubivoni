Katie Pipers acid attacker fled the country over two months ago

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Thursday, February 1, 2024

Katie Piper's acid attacker is believed to have fled the country more than two months ago, police admitted on Monday.

Stefan Sylvestre, 34, was granted parole in 2020, 11 years after the assault that left Ms Piper with severe burns to her face.

It emerged on Sunday that Sylvestre was on the run after being recalled to prison for breaching the conditions of his licence.

Now police have conceded that they believe the convict left the country on August 2.

The Metropolitan Police are leading the investigation into his whereabouts, alongside partner agencies thought to include the National Crime Agency, according to The Mirror.

A spokesman for Scotland Yard said on Monday: "Officers are appealing for information to find a man wanted for recall to prison.

"Police were notified on Friday, 23 September, that Stefan Sylvestre, 34, had been recalled to prison. His last known address was in north London.

"Enquiries undertaken so far indicate that Sylvestre left the UK on Tuesday, 2 August. Officers from the Met's Specialist Crime Command continue to work with partner agencies to locate and arrest him. Sylvestre should not be approached."

Sylvestre, who was then 19, attacked Ms Piper in Golders Green, North London, with acid in 2008 on the orders of her obsessive ex-boyfriend Daniel Lynch.

He was jailed for life the following year, with a minimum term of six years.

Lynch was jailed for life with a minimum of 16 years for sexually assaulting Ms Piper and telling Sylvestre to throw acid over her. He cannot be considered for release until 2025.

Lynch and Ms Piper had dated briefly before the steroid-fuelled martial arts fan, who had a previous conviction for pouring boiling water over a man, became obsessively jealous.

In court, Lynch and Sylvestre were told by Judge Nicholas Browne QC they were "the face of pure evil".

In a victim impact statement to the court, Ms Piper wrote: "When the acid was thrown at me, it felt like I was burning in hell. It was an indescribable, unique, torturous pain.

"I have lost my future, my career, my spirit, my body, my looks, my dignityâ€¦ the list goes on. All I am left with is an empty shell. A part of me has died that will never come back. This is worse than death."

Sylvestre became eligible for parole in 2015 but his application was blocked after he was deemed unfit for release.

Another application in 2018 was successful and he was freed.

At the time Ms Piper had tweeted: "This is a really difficult time for me.

"I am trying to come to terms with the decision and this is something I need to deal with.

"Over the past two weeks Strictly (Come Dancing) has already given me such a welcome and positive distraction from my past.

"Whilst there is never a good time to hear this news, I am glad I have this new journey to concentrate on. Thank you for your continued support. Love Katie."

In 2019, Sylvestre was recalled to jail for an unrelated incident.

Sylvestre was granted parole again in 2020 and was released after errors in his prosecution emerged.

It is not clear why Sylvestre has again been recalled to prison.

Offenders who are released from jail are subject to strict licence conditions, which can include exclusion zones. Failure to comply can result in an immediate recall to prison.

Piper, 38, who now works as a TV presenter, was awarded an OBE in the 2022 New Year Honours for services to charity and victims of burns and other disfigurement injuries.

She is best known for sharing her story about surviving the attack in the documentary Katie: My Beautiful Face.
