Albert Pujols Biography

Albert Pujols was raised primarily by his grandmother in Santo Domingo. He comes from a large family with ten uncles and aunts. He is his mother and father’s only child. He was born to softball pitcher Bienvenido Pujols (father). He had a bad habit of drinking. Albert frequently drove his father home after getting drunk. As he grew older, he began to play baseball with limes as balls and a milk carton as a glove. Albert, his father, and his grandmother moved to Washington Heights, New York City, in 1996. They relocated to Independence, Missouri, after two months to join a few relatives.

Albert was an avid baseball player at Fort Osage High School when he was in Independence. He was regarded as an “All-State athlete” by all. He received a baseball scholarship to Maple Woods Community College after graduating from high school in December 1998.

Albert was selected by the Cardinals in the thirteenth round of the 1999 MLB draft after a season of college baseball. He played for the Cardinals and helped them win the World Series twice, in 2006 and 2011.

Career

Albert became the youngest player in NL history to win the batting title in 2003. He also received his first ‘Sporting News Player of the Year Award,’ as well as his second ‘Silver Slugger Award.’

He signed a seven-year, $100 million contract with the St. Louis Cardinals in 2004. He was the first player to receive the ‘Fielding Bible Award’ for his “first base” position that season in 2006.

He won the NL MVP and the Silver Slugger Award in 2008. He received the ‘Roberto Clemente Award for his sportsmanship. 2013 was not a good year for him. However, in a game against the ‘Washington Nationals’ in 2014, he regained his form and hit his 500th home run. He became the third-youngest player to reach the milestone.

Albert Pujols Wife, Marriage

On January 1, 2000, Albert married Deidre. The couple has five children: Isabella (an earlier relationship’s daughter), Sophia, Esther Grace, Ezra, and Albert Jr. The family lives in St. Louis during the offseason. Isabella was born with Down syndrome. Albert and Deidre are advocates for those born with this condition. He became a US citizen in 2007 after passing his citizenship test with a perfect score of 100.

Albert also contributed to the “Mike Coolbaugh Memorial Tournament” in 2009. When the event sponsor backed out in the middle, his donation helped the event organizer run the event.

Albert opened “Pujols 5 Westport Grill” in Westport Plaza, Maryland Heights, Missouri, in 2006. Albert joined the St. Louis Soccer United group in 2008, which was planning to bring an MLS (Major League Soccer) franchise to the St. Louis area.

Albert and his wife Deidre Pujols established the Pujols Family Foundation in 2005. This foundation is a non-profit organization that raises awareness about “Down syndrome” and provides assistance to families who have loved ones who have this illness. In addition, the organization helps the poor in the Dominican Republic. This foundation also hosts an annual “All Stars Basketball Game” for players with Down syndrome. Missouri Baptist University hosts the event.
